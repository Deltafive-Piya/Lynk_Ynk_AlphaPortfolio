Post-Care For Your Tattoo

After 2 hours, remove and dispose of bandages. You will not need to reapply any bandages to allow open healing.

Wash your new tattoo using warm water and gentle antibacterial soap. Avoid hot water as it will further irritate your skin.

Gently pat your tattoo dry with a clean towel. Never rub or scrub your healing tattoo. Paper towel is recommended to avoid cloth towel lint and possible bacterial infection.

Apply a thin layer of A&D ointment and rub into skin in a circular motion to allow skin absorption. Apply ointment 3-4 times a day for the first week.

After your first week of healing, you may switch to unscented lotions and only apply as needed (when your tattoo looks dry and dehydrated)

Your tattoo will itch during the healing process. Do not scratch or pick at any flaking skin especially if you have blood scabs.

Avoid excessive sun exposure and do not submerge your tattoo in water while it is healing.

Protect your new tattoos with
Recovery Derma Shield

Benefits:

  • Light, latex-free, transparent matte film bandage
  • Eliminates scabbing and reduces risk of scarring
  • Maximum breathability and flexibility
  • Waterproof and permeable to allow perspiration

Directions for Use:

  1. Gently clean with a light antibacterial soap with warm water and pat dry the tattoo and surrounding area, ensuring there is no soap or any residue on the skin.
  2. Cut the Derm Shield to cover the tattooed area plus a 1 to 2 inch margin from the edge of the tattoo.
  3. Peel off backing to expose the adhesive side and position the Derm Shield over the tattoo with the adhesive side facing the skin.
  4. Gently apply using light pressure, starting from the center to the edges to eliminate air bubbles. Do not stretch the Derm Shield during application.
  5. The first Derm Shield application should stay in place for 24 hours, depending on the level of exudation. It is normal for blood and fluids to collect under the bandage.
  6. A second application can be used for several days, and a third application may be used if necessary. Gently clean and dry the area between applications. Do not wear any piece longer than one week.

Directions for Removal:

It is recommended to remove Derm Shield in the shower. The water will help with the removal process. Gently peel the Derm Shield by pulling it slowly from the edge back over itself. Do not pull the bandage upwards.

Caution:

For external use only. Use only as directed. Do not apply to infected wounds. This product contains adhesives and may cause allergic reactions. If an allergic reaction or any irritation occurs, discontinue use immediately. Consult a physician if the reaction or irritation is severe.
